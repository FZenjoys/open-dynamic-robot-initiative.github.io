Open Dynamic Robot Initiative

An Open Torque-Controlled Modular Robot Architecture for Legged Locomotion Research

Introduction

We present an open-source leg architecture for dynamic legged robot research. We designed a simple and modular actuator-design concept leveraging standard plastic 3D printing and off-the-shelf parts. Our proposed concept allows for rapid distribution and duplication, with little engineering know-how. The light-weight system design enables inexpensive yet high performance force and effective force-control control, up to 11.3 dimensionless leg stiffness without active damping. Its robustness and powerful actuation allows for stark impacts from drops and dynamic locomotion maneuvers. A single leg is actuated by a low-geared but powerful brushless DC actuator module, allowing up to 3.4 Nm joint torque, and 17 N effective (virtual leg length) force. Assembled from its modular actuator nodes, we present the quadruped robot Solo, its locomotion capabilities and dexterity. With a weight of 2.2 kg, Solo is the most light-weight force-controlled modular quadruped robot, to the best of our knowledge. This feature makes it intrinsically safe and ideal for academic research and education: it requires less peak power to locomote, and it can safely be handled by a single researcher.
